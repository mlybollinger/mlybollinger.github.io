Portfolio

Socioeconomic Causes of Heart Disease

Combining data from the U.S. Census, the Center for Disease Control's Mortality Data, and Medicare's Hospital Compare Data, I built a linear regression model to identify important factors in the occurrence and treatment of heart disease. I also clustered data with DBSCAN to find that a set of counties in northern Louisiana and Mississippi were outliers in heart disease mortality. From these observations, I formulated an action plan to reduce heart disease fatalities nationally on an affordable budget.

Consulting for TrackMaven

Our General Assembly team was assigned by TrackMaven, a marketing analytics firm, to analyze data from tweets by Conde Nast brands to identify how those brands could strengthen engagement. I discovered positive and negative outliers in brand engagement by plotting engagement for each brand against the logarithm of the brands' follower counts. I used Latent Dirichlet Allocation to group the text data generated by an image classifier from media attached to tweets into different topics, which we used to find predictors of engagement. We delivered a 20-minute presentation on our findings and recommendations to TrackMaven professionals.

Modeling Outbreaks of West Nile Virus

Given data from mosquito traps across Chicago from 2007 to 2013, our team built a classification model to predict whether West Nile would be present in a given trap. I conducted exploratory data analysis on weather data and assisted in creating an XGBoost model that predicted West Nile presence with over 70% accuracy.
